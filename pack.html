EnhanceConf will be the first conference dedicated to progressive enhancement.

We aim to bring together some of the top minds in web development, providing a unique opportunity to discuss techniques, share research, gain feedback and build relationships.

About

Supporting the devices of 3 billion users can seem daunting. We believe progressive enhancement gives a massive advantage and remains an important aspect in pushing the boundaries of the web.

“After attending a number of conferences showcasing the latest frameworks I felt something was missing.

I wanted a conference covering the state of the art in progressive enhancement; focussing on techniques that provide the best experience to the widest possible audience.”

Simon McManus - Organiser

Topics we want to cover include:

  • Device/Network agnostic design
  • Isomorphic apps
  • Offline apps
  • Web Components
  • Application architectures
  • Isomorphic apps
  • Offline apps
  • Device/network-agnostic design
  • Progressive Apps
  • Web Components

We may dive into a particular language, library or framework but the focus will be on techniques rather than specific implementations.

EnhanceConf will be a community event, any extra money from ticket sales will be put back into the event.

Format

The day will consist of four sessions, each with three 20 minute talks. At the end of each session, Jeremy Keith will chair a panel with the session’s speakers, fielding questions from the audience and social media.

Venue

One day in 2016 150 attendees will gather in the Great Room at RSA House.

Conveniently located just off The Strand RSA House is a short walk from Charing Cross, Embankment and Leicester Square London underground stations.

The Great Room is dominated by the epic series of paintings "Progress of Human Knowledge and Culture" by James Barry and was the room in which RSA fellow Alexander Graham Bell gave the first practical demonstration of his new invention, the telephone.
